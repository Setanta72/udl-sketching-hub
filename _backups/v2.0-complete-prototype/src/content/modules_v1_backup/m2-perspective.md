## 1. Core Concepts

- **Horizon Line (HL):** Represents the viewer's eye level.

- **Vanishing Points (VP):** The points on the HL where parallel lines converge.

- **1, 2, and 3-Point Perspective.**

## 6. Application

Create a simple 'street view' scene.

- Draw an HL and two VPs.
- Sketch 3-5 'buildings' (as simple cubes) on the same ground plane.

## 7. Mastery Rubric

### For Guided Path Learners:

- **Novice:** Lines are parallel (isometric) or diverge. Relies 100% on VPs.
- **Proficient:** Can accurately construct complex scenes _using on-page VPs and rulers_.
- **Mastery:** Can rapidly produce complex, accurate drawings _using perspective grids or guides_.

### For Mastery Path Learners:

- **Novice:** Lines are parallel (isometric) or diverge. Relies 100% on VPs.
- **Proficient:** Can estimate simple forms freehand, but with some inconsistencies.
- **Mastery:** Intuitively sketches complex forms in accurate gestural 2-point perspective _without any guides_.
